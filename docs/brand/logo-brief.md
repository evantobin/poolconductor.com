# Logo Identity

## Selected Direction: Open Channel

![Open Channel monogram](./concepts/open-channel-monogram.png)

The Pool Conductor mark is a compact geometric monogram built from one solid
shape and one open channel of negative space.

The outer form reads as a capital `P` for Pool. A capital `C` is cut through its
upper body for Conductor. The two initials are not placed beside or on top of one
another; they share the same geometry. That relationship is the central brand
idea: existing pool equipment and its control system become one understandable
whole.

## What the Form Communicates

- The solid `P` gives the mark a clear connection to the product category.
- The negative-space `C` identifies the coordinating role of Pool Conductor.
- The `C` remains open to the right, representing compatibility with different
  Matter systems rather than a closed platform.
- Positive and negative space depend on each other, just as the controller and
  connected equipment remain distinct but work as one system.
- The single continuous silhouette communicates stability and local operation.
- The open center keeps the mark from feeling heavy and makes it recognizable at
  small sizes.

The logo does not try to diagram pumps, relays, sensors, or smart-home platforms.
Those details belong in product messaging. The mark expresses the larger promise:
Pool Conductor connects the pool to the way the customer already controls their
home.

## Why It Fits the Brand

The mark is modern without looking futuristic. Its geometry feels appropriate
on a controller enclosure, in a smart-home interface, on an installer vehicle,
or in a printed manual. It is compact, direct, and easy to recognize without
using a mascot or a familiar pool-industry cliché.

For homeowners, it looks like a finished consumer product rather than a project
board. For pool professionals, it has enough weight and simplicity to sit beside
established equipment brands on an installation.

## Construction

- Begin with a bold geometric capital `P` in a nearly square footprint.
- Use one strong vertical stem and one broad upper bowl.
- Cut a geometric `C` through the bowl using negative space.
- Keep the `C` open on the right with a clear horizontal channel.
- Match the inner and outer curve tension so the two letters feel designed as
  one form.
- Use modest optical corner softening. Corners should feel precise, not sharp or
  playful.
- Maintain enough negative space for the `C` to remain open at favicon size.
- Do not place the symbol inside a separate box for the primary logo.

The approved raster concept records the direction, not final production
geometry. Final artwork should be redrawn as a clean SVG on a consistent grid.

## Wordmark

Pair the symbol with the plain title-case wordmark `Pool Conductor`. Use the same
neutral sans-serif family as the site in a medium or semibold weight.

The wordmark should not repeat the monogram's idea with custom joins, water
effects, or altered letters. The symbol provides recognition; the wordmark
provides clarity.

Preferred lockups:

- Horizontal: symbol to the left of `Pool Conductor`
- Stacked: symbol above the centered wordmark
- Symbol-only: favicon, app icon, hardware stamp, and small labels

## Color

The logo is always one solid color.

- Preferred light-background version: Equipment Ink `#1D2A2B`
- Reversed version: white on Equipment Ink
- Single-color production: black, white, or one available spot color
- Optional digital accent version: Pool Aqua `#187F91`

The mark must never depend on color to reveal the `P` or `C`.

## Clear Space

Use the width of the `P` stem as the minimum clear space on every side. No text,
border, photograph, or equipment label should enter that area.

When paired with the wordmark, use approximately half the stem width between the
symbol and the first letter. Adjust optically at small sizes.

## Minimum Size

- Digital symbol: 24px high
- Digital horizontal lockup: 120px wide
- Printed symbol: 8mm high
- Enclosure or vehicle use: scale freely while preserving clear space

## Reproduction Requirements

- Exactly one solid color
- No gradients, opacity, shading, texture, or shadows
- One continuous positive shape
- One principal negative-space channel
- No tiny counters or detached pieces
- Suitable for vinyl cutting, screen printing, embroidery, embossing, and laser
  engraving
- SVG is the source artwork; PNG and WebP files are exports
- Provide full, reversed, horizontal, stacked, and symbol-only versions

## Avoid

- Adding waves, ripples, droplets, or pool silhouettes inside the mark
- Turning the open `C` into a closed ring
- Adding device ports, nodes, arrows, or circuit traces
- Putting the primary symbol inside a rounded-square app tile
- Stretching or condensing the symbol
- Outlining the solid form
- Using separate colors for the `P` and `C`
- Adding a conductor, baton, music notation, or other literal name reference
- Pairing it with decorative, script, or futuristic typography

## Evaluation Questions

- Can the `P` be recognized before the mark is explained?
- Does the negative space clearly read as an open `C`?
- Does the mark remain balanced at 24px?
- Does it look dependable before it looks clever?
- Is it equally strong in black, white, and Equipment Ink?
- Would a homeowner remember it after seeing it once?
- Would a pool professional put it on a finished installation?
- Can it remain useful as the product supports more equipment and platforms?
