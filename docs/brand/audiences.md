# Audiences

Pool Conductor serves two audiences with different jobs but the same desired
result: a pool that works without becoming a daily project.

## Homeowners

### What They Want

- A pool that is ready when they want to use it
- Familiar controls in the smart-home app they already use
- Fewer trips to the equipment pad
- Confidence that schedules and equipment are doing what they should
- No new subscription or separate app to babysit
- A clear answer when something needs attention

### What They Worry About

- Pool automation that is expensive and confusing
- A system only the installer knows how to use
- Losing control when the internet or a cloud service is unavailable
- New hardware that requires replacing equipment that still works
- Technology that creates alerts instead of solving problems

### What Builds Trust

- Plain descriptions of what the controller does
- Compatibility explained in recognizable equipment terms
- Real photos of the controller and a clean installation
- A simple view of current pool status
- Local control and no required recurring service
- Evidence that a pool professional can install and support it

### Core Message

Your pool stays ready, and you spend less time managing the equipment behind it.

## Pool Professionals

This includes pool builders, service companies, repair technicians, and
independent installers.

### What They Want

- A controller that installs cleanly with equipment already in the field
- Straightforward wiring, commissioning, and customer handoff
- Remote information that helps diagnose a problem before a truck roll
- Fewer calls about schedules, pump speed, and basic equipment status
- A modern upgrade they can offer without replacing an entire pad
- Customers who understand and value the finished installation

### What They Worry About

- Being made responsible for unreliable consumer technology
- Extra configuration time that erases the margin on a job
- Customers changing advanced settings and creating callbacks
- Unclear compatibility or incomplete documentation
- A vendor competing with their service relationship

### What Builds Trust

- A clear installation path and good diagrams
- Predictable local behavior
- Useful diagnostics without constant noise
- Separate homeowner and advanced controls
- Honest compatibility boundaries
- A company that treats the professional as the customer's long-term partner

### Core Message

Give customers simple control and give yourself a system that is easier to hand
off, understand, and support.

## Shared Outcome

The homeowner should feel that the pool takes care of itself. The pool
professional should know that the system is behaving exactly as intended.

The brand should never frame those interests as competing. A happier customer
and a more supportable installation are the same win.
